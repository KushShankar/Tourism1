MUSSOORIE



Landmarks


Mall Road

The Mall also has a Methodist church and the Tibetan market place nearby makes for a diverse cultural mix.

Lal Tibba


The place is famous for a Japanese telescope which was placed here in 1967.

Kempty Fall
The curious name of this waterfall is derived from the words ‘camp-tea’, which was popularised by British tea parties here during the colonial era.

Cloud End


Cloud End is surrounded by thick deodar forest.

Lake Mist
About 5 km (3 mi) before Kempty Falls on the Mussoorie-Kempty road is Lake Mist, through which flows the Kempty river with its numerous small waterfalls.

Benog Wildlife Sanctuary

This one is about 11 kilometers from Library Point but still interesting for those who like bird watching and the wildlife.
